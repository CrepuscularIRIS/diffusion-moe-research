Your diagnosis is pointing at the right hole, but I'd sharpen it, because as stated it contains a trap. Let me separate the two things you've bundled.

**The real missing abstraction: the system has writes but no compounding read.** Look at what v1.0 already produces: the conversion law emits a constraint on every death, the anomaly ledger logs every surprise, region-closes record why territory died. The raw material of intuition is being generated every cycle. But look at where it lands — scattered across individual tree-node insights, episode-scoped, with no retrieval structure. And the one durable bank the system does have (taste-operators) is *deliberately de-domained*, because it was built to fix monoculture, which is a breadth problem. So the system can accumulate generic moves and per-episode facts, but there is no object where evidence compounds *per territory*. Human intuition is exactly that object: a posterior over one domain that has hardened into a prior on the next problem in that domain. Taste.md defines taste as a prior over information-per-effort — but the four axes only ever install *generic* priors. The architecture structurally cannot form a domain-specific one. That's the missing piece, and "failure atlas" is the right name for it.

This also explains the lateral drift as a symptom rather than a separate bug. Region-close, menu-exhaustion fixes, and surprise accounting all push outward, and nothing pushes back — because staying has no legible asset. The value of staying is superlinear (each constraint re-prices every future candidate in that territory), but the per-cycle taste objective can't see the compounding term, so a fresh problem always looks competitive. Once the atlas exists, the counter-force appears for free: a territory with a dense atlas makes /forge measurably cheaper and sharper there, and that shows up in the mechanism-clarity ÷ cost ranking without adding any scoring machinery.

**The trap in your framing: "continuously download surveys and read key papers" is not depth — it's more breadth-shaped input.** The system's own taste model already names why: literature mining interpolates the field's consensus; your own anomalies are the only structurally off-distribution data you possess. A subsystem that continuously acquires and reads papers is also the ceremony failure mode wearing a scholar's coat — reading-as-productivity, process artifacts as outputs. Human researchers who "stay with one problem" do read deeply, but the load-bearing part of their intuition is *I've run two hundred experiments here and know which knobs matter*. The paper-reading is secondary and just-in-time — a pattern v1.0 already has (the operator source-paper read before /prereg). So: literature depth folds into the atlas as lower-grade entries mined on demand; it does not justify an acquisition subsystem.

**The minimal fix, under the anti-accretion rule** — this is a rule-2 case (an existing piece absorbs it), zero new commands, reusing the operator-bank pattern exactly (project-side markdown, outside the parts budget):

1. **One file per territory, `atlas/<territory>.md`.** Entry = one line: the claim/constraint · evidence pointer (run artifact or paper) · evidence grade (own-run vs literature) · what it re-prices. Own-run entries are the spine; literature entries are admitted at lower grade.
2. **/autopsy write-through redirects:** conversion-law outputs and [ANOMALY] lines append to the territory's atlas, not only the node insight. A region-close writes an *epitaph entry* — "dead because X; would reopen if Y" — which makes lateral moves reversible and informed instead of amnesiac.
3. **/prospect and /forge read the territory's atlas FIRST** — before the mines, before the palettes. In /prospect it's effectively Mine 0; in /forge it's the constraint set that re-prices candidates before any design happens, and mature-territory MECHANISM/KILL fields should start citing atlas entries.

That's roughly six lines of edits across two skills plus a five-line entry schema. The atlas is not a fifth axis (four is the ceiling, and axes are generic priors); it's the thing the axes were always supposed to be retrieved *through* — the domain instantiation of the prior.

How you'd know it worked, in the system's own currency: surprise-per-GPU-hour in a stayed territory should beat fresh territories after a few cycles, and /forge candidates should increasingly kill or reshape themselves against atlas entries *before* runs — evidence re-pricing plans pre-spend is precisely "intuition emerging," made observable.

One honest caveat: the atlas creates a new failure surface — atlas-worship, where stale or wrongly-scoped entries veto live candidates the way v0.4's occupancy scan did. The one-line inoculation: atlas entries re-price, they never veto, and an entry contradicted by a fresh run is itself an [ANOMALY] — the atlas must stay falsifiable by the loop it feeds.
